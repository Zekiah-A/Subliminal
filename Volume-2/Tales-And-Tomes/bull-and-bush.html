Bull and Bush - By Angelos

Oben den Hgel, liegt es den alten Bull & Bush
A Bush and a Bull, cant see them around,
Just in gothic writing, on the north facing pub.

So where are they hidden -
The Bush and the Bull?
The toilets, the kitchen, or under the Mll?
The white building? Unlikely
For beasts that mighty.
But maybe Im wrong.

The bull was me, for it climbed the bush,
In an electrical substation; eternal damnation,
Danger of death! 415 volts.
But you wont die; it's between screws and bolts.

For it seems like a tube station
Rather than a substation,
Clickety clack, knew twas that!
Artificial crescendo; where are they off to?
Air turbulence shows, it blows to the north.

This dust has a familiar smell;
The smell of decay, the stench of rocks.
Its appeared on the headlines countless times
For its particulates are known for scarring lungs.
Yet who can resist this landmark of London?

Turning right towards the circular shaft,
Hier liegt ein Sign der sagt:
Depth of shaft: 38 metres
My wheel touches the first stair of the shaft.
Spiralling down in a spiral of decay,
Like Mr Statins might; I picture its plight.

Slamming the breaks, bike
Crashed on the landing,
Just like the empire of OzyAndy.

Rushing down to platform level,
A train whizzed south from north; a furious rebel.
Its destination read express to history
of the Hills largest port.
Once a trading spot; largest depot,
At the worlds poshest school,
now among the level sands.

Like a dead old bull decaying in a bush,
OzyAndys statue crumbles,
On what once was a platform.
Reduced to rubbles.
